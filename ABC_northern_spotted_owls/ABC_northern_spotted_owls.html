Take Action For The Northern Spotted Owl And

Its Old-Growth Forest Habitat







Please write to President Obama, administration officials, and your Members of Congress today and urge them to support stronger conservation measures for the endangered Northern Spotted Owl. The Northwest Forest Plan which protects the owl's habitat is up for revision, and your voice is needed to keep its protections for the owl and the threatened Marbled Murrelet.


The U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service recently announced a status review of the Northern Spotted Owl to determine if it should be considered an "endangered species" as opposed to threatened. This is a result of new studies showing the owl's population is declining, and that it is sinking dangerously close to extinction.


Meanwhile, the Forest Service and Bureau of Land Management are preparing to update forest plans that are managed under the Northwest Forest Plan which was put in place twenty years ago to protect the owl's habitat, and restore the old growth forest ecosystem. The plan is working to slow the owl's decline and its protections need to be maintained as the forest plans are updated.


Your voice is urgently needed in support of saving the Northern Spotted Owl and Marbled Murrelet, and ensure that the many benefits of the Northwest Forest Plan are not lost as the forest management plans are revised.
